A Journey Through Time

The History of Our Museum Our museum has been a cornerstone of discovery and education since its founding in 1925. Initially a modest collection of local artifacts, it has grown into a world-class institution housing thousands of fascinating items that tell the story of our planet and its incredible history. One of our most renowned exhibits is our fossil collection, which began in the 1930s with excavations in the Badlands of North America. Over the years, our paleontology team has expanded the collection with finds from the Gobi Desert, the Sahara, and even underwater digs in the Baltic Sea. These fossils give visitors a glimpse into prehistoric life, from towering dinosaurs to ancient marine creatures. Our museum is also home to unique cultural artifacts, thanks to partnerships with indigenous communities around the world. In the 1950s, we collaborated with South American tribes to preserve ceremonial artifacts, and in the 1980s, we established a groundbreaking agreement with Arctic communities to document and share their heritage. Today, our museum stands as a bridge between the past and present, fostering curiosity and understanding for all ages. Each exhibit tells a story, inviting visitors to embark on their own journey of discovery.
